# Chronology of the Hundred Days' Reform Edicts (June 11 - Sept 21, 1898)

To truly understand the political panic that gripped Peking, one must look at the pacing of Emperor Guangxu's edicts. What started as reasonable educational reforms rapidly accelerated into a frantic, aggressive dismantling of the entire Manchu power structure within a mere 103 days.

## Phase 1: The Educational Shock
*The initial weeks focused heavily on reforming the mind of the nation.*

* **June 11, 1898 (Day 1): The Declaration** 
  * Emperor Guangxu issues the landmark decree "On National Policies," officially announcing that the Qing Empire must adopt modern Western learning to survive.
* **June 13, 1898 (Day 3): Abolition of the Eight-Legged Essay** 
  * A catastrophic shock to the gentry. The Emperor abolishes the traditional, centuries-old Confucian essay format for the civil service exams, replacing it with practical essays on current events and science. Millions of traditional scholars instantly realize their life's work is useless.
* **June 15, 1898 (Day 5): Cixi’s First Counter-Move** 
  * Not an edict by the Emperor, but a forced concession. Cixi forces Guangxu to dismiss his moderate tutor (Weng Tonghe) and appoints her loyalist, General Ronglu, as Viceroy of Zhili, quietly taking control of the military around Peking.
* **June 23, 1898 (Day 13): Abolition of Traditional Academies** 
  * An edict orders that all traditional academies and local shrines across the empire be converted into modern schools teaching a Western curriculum.
* **July 3, 1898 (Day 23): The Imperial University** 
  * Edict establishing the *Jingshi Daxuetang* (The Imperial University of Peking) to serve as the pinnacle of the new modern educational system. (This would be the only major reform to survive the coup).
* **July 5, 1898 (Day 25): Military Modernization** 
  * Edicts are issued ordering all provincial armies to abandon traditional bows, spears, and outdated tactics, and immediately begin drilling with modern Western firearms.

## Phase 2: Economic and Infrastructure Push
*Mid-summer focused on attempting to rapidly build a modern, capitalist economy to fend off foreign monopolies.*

* **July 12 - August 9, 1898 (Days 32 - 60): Industrial Edicts** 
  * A flurry of decrees are issued establishing central bureaus to oversee railways, mining, and agriculture. 
  * Edicts officially encourage private citizens to form capitalist enterprises and modern chambers of commerce.
  * Edicts order the establishment of modern naval academies and the rebuilding of the Beiyang Fleet.

## Phase 3: The Political Purge and the Collapse
*In late summer, frustrated by conservative obstructionism, the Emperor turns his edicts toward dismantling the government itself. This is what triggered the coup.*

* **August 30, 1898 (Day 81): The Abolition of Sinecures** 
  * **The Fatal Edict:** Guangxu abolishes several central government bureaus and thousands of redundant provincial posts (such as the governorships of Hubei, Guangdong, and Yunnan, which already had Viceroys). 
  * He slashes the traditional stipends paid to the Manchu Bannermen. This directly threatens the wealth and survival of the Manchu nobility, driving them en masse to Cixi to beg for her intervention.
* **September 5, 1898 (Day 87): Bypassing the Grand Council** 
  * Realizing the senior conservative ministers are sabotaging his orders, Guangxu appoints four radical young reformers (Tan Sitong, Lin Xu, Yang Rui, Liu Guangdi) as special secretaries to the Grand Council. They are granted unprecedented power to draft edicts directly, completely humiliating and bypassing the old guard.
* **September 11, 1898 (Day 93): The Right to Petition** 
  * Guangxu decrees that any commoner or low-ranking official may submit memorials (petitions) directly to the Emperor in closed, sealed envelopes. This destroys the traditional gatekeeping power of the conservative censors.
* **September 14, 1898 (Day 96): The Japanese Advisor** 
  * It is announced that former Japanese Prime Minister Ito Hirobumi will be hired as a paramount foreign advisor to guide the Qing government. The Manchu court views this as outright treason.
* **September 18, 1898 (Day 100): The Secret Plot** 
  * Recognizing Cixi is about to strike, the Emperor secretly summons Yuan Shikai. That night, Tan Sitong begs Yuan to use his modern army to assassinate Ronglu and besiege Cixi.
* **September 20, 1898 (Day 102): The Betrayal** 
  * Yuan Shikai betrays the reformers to Ronglu.
* **September 21, 1898 (Day 103): The Wuxu Coup** 
  * Empress Dowager Cixi launches her palace coup. Emperor Guangxu is imprisoned. Within days, Cixi issues her own edicts, violently reversing almost every single reform listed above and ordering the execution of the reformist leaders.
