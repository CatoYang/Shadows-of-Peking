# The Chronicle Bible: Shadows of Peking

## Overarching Metaplot
The history of Kindred in China in this chronicle is deeply tied to the rise and fall of mortal dynasties, heavily influenced by the Manchu conquest and later western imperialism. 

### Key Historical Pillars
1. **The Vaulderie Pact & The Qing Conquest (1618–1644)**: Jurchen/Manchu vampires (largely Tzimisce and Gangrel) utilized Vaulderie-like blood rituals to break free from their elders in Manchuria. They invaded Ming China alongside their mortal counterparts, conquering the land and devastated local han vampires
2. Of the manchu vampires, some stayed in manchuria, and relocated to peking and some continued onto the southern territories.
3. The Vampires of the Ming Empire suffered, the court at peking that were of han vampires were mostly destroyed, some survived by fleeing and some escaped into torpor. Those that escaped eventually joined the anarch vampires but were persecuted over the centuaries. 
4. **The Queue Order & Oppression (1645–onward)**: To flush out hidden Han vampires, the Manchu Kindred enforced the Queue Order. Waking every evening to shave their foreheads made Han vampires extremely vulnerable, allowing the Manchu Kindred to systematically hunt them. Gate controls in Peking further restricted Han vampire movement at night.
5. **Internal Infighting**: The Manchu Vampires established a new vampiric society that mimicked a camarillian court but subsequently devolved into infighting to control the Qing Emperor and Court.
6. **Racial Evolution of the court**: As the ages pass and with a disparity in populations, there were more han chinese vampires integrated into court as well as mixed han-manchu vampires, blurring the racial lines but the customs still favor the elder manchus.
7. Oboi Purges 1661: Younger neonates frustrated with the tower attempt to seize power but were brutally purged
8. Yongzheng purges 1722: Again political infighting caused another disruption in the tower. More elders and neonates get purged
9. Anarch Purges 1750: The literary purges of 1750 trigger deep resentment from the anarch brujah whom are tied to han who were involved. The Qing subsequently used this as a smokescreen to target anarch kindred.
10. **The Taiping Rebellion & Christian Vampires (1850–1864)**: A massive holy war influenced by Christian Kindred that crippled the Qing's hold on the South and planted seeds for future religious/kindred conflict.
11. **Arrival of the European Vampires (1860–onward)**: Following the Opium Wars and the establishment of foreign legations in Peking. Foreign vampires arrived into court with an elevated status being aligned with foreign powers.
12. **Arrival of Camarillian Dignitaries**: The western camarilla send envoys to attempt to induct peking into the global camarillian organisation.
13. **The Reform Movement & The Wuxu Coup (1898)**: The Peking Kindred Court functions similarly to a Camarilla Praxis but is divided. Reformer neonates and ancillae pushed for modernization based on western ideas, but were brutally purged by conservative Manchu elders during the Wuxu Coup.
14. **The Boxer Rebellion (1899–1901)**: The current boiling point. The anti-foreign, anti-Christian mortal uprising provides the perfect cover for the Qing elders to purge the European Camarilla and Chinese Christian vampires (the "Er Maozi").
