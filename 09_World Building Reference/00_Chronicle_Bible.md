# The Chronicle Bible: Shadows of Peking

## Overarching Metaplot
The history of Kindred in China in this chronicle is deeply tied to the rise and fall of mortal dynasties, heavily influenced by the Manchu conquest and later western imperialism. 

### Key Historical Pillars
1. **The Vaulderie Pact & The Qing Conquest (1618–1644)**: Jurchen/Manchu vampires (largely Tzimisce and Gangrel) utilized Vaulderie-like blood rituals to break free from their elders in Manchuria. They invaded Ming China alongside their mortal counterparts, conquering the land and wiping out local Han Kindred.
2. **The Queue Order & Oppression (1645–onward)**: To flush out hidden Han vampires, the Manchu Kindred enforced the Queue Order. Waking every evening to shave their foreheads made Han vampires extremely vulnerable, allowing the Manchu Kindred to systematically hunt them. Gate controls in Peking further restricted Han vampire movement at night.
3. **The Taiping Rebellion & Christian Vampires (1850–1864)**: A massive holy war influenced by Christian Kindred that crippled the Qing's hold on the South and planted seeds for future religious/kindred conflict.
4. **Arrival of the Camarilla (1860–onward)**: Following the Opium Wars and the establishment of foreign legations in Peking, European vampires (Camarilla envoys) arrived to induct the Chinese Kindred into the Ivory Tower.
5. **The Reform Movement & The Wuxu Coup (1898)**: The Peking Kindred Court functions similarly to a Camarilla Praxis but is divided. Reformer neonates and ancillae pushed for modernization based on western ideas, but were brutally purged by conservative Manchu elders during the Wuxu Coup.
6. **The Boxer Rebellion (1899–1901)**: The current boiling point. The anti-foreign, anti-Christian mortal uprising provides the perfect cover for the Qing elders to purge the European Camarilla and Chinese Christian vampires (the "Er Maozi").

## General Guidelines
- **No Kuei-jin**: The setting uses standard western Kindred clans adapted to the historical Chinese context.
- **History First**: Kindred plots are woven into, and explained by, accurate real-world historical events.
