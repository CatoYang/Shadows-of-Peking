# Yongzheng Succession and Purges

**Date:** 1722–1735

## Historical Context
The Yongzheng Emperor ascended the throne following a highly secretive and heavily contested succession battle with his brothers (the "Nine Lords' War"). His reign was marked by intense centralization of power, the creation of the Grand Council, and a profound reliance on secret police and spies. He ruthlessly purged his siblings and their supporters, executing or imprisoning many high-ranking officials to ensure his absolute authority.

## Kindred Implications
The mortal succession crisis mirrored a devastating shadow war within the Qing Kindred Court. Factions of Manchu vampires backed different mortal princes, leading to unprecedented infighting. The victors, aligning with Yongzheng, utilized the mortal secret police to hunt down rival Kindred. Many prominent elders were forced into torpor or fled the capital entirely during this period. The resulting paranoia permanently altered the culture of the Peking Praxis, turning it into a court where trust is nonexistent and every shadow hides an informant.
