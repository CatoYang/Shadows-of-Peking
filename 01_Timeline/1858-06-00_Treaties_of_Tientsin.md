# Treaties of Tientsin (June 1858)

After Anglo-French forces easily captured the Dagu Forts and the city of Tianjin (Tientsin), the terrified Qing Court was forced to sign a series of devastatingly unequal treaties.

## The Humiliating Terms
* **Foreign Legations in Peking:** The Qing were forced to allow foreign diplomats to reside permanently in the capital city of Peking. This was considered a supreme insult to Imperial sovereignty.
* **Missionary Expansion:** Foreign missionaries were granted the legal right to travel and proselytize anywhere in the Chinese interior, deeply disrupting rural social structures.
* **Legalization of Opium:** The opium trade, the devastating drug that had caused the first war, was officially legalized.
* **New Treaty Ports:** Several new ports along the coast and up the Yangtze River were forcibly opened to foreign trade and extraterritoriality.

## The Aftermath
* The Qing Court bitterly regretted signing the treaties and refused to ratify them the following year, leading to renewed fighting, the eventual march on Peking, and the burning of the Old Summer Palace in 1860.
