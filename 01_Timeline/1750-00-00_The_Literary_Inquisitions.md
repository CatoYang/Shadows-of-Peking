# The Literary Inquisitions (18th Century)

During the height of the Qing Dynasty's power (under the Kangxi, Yongzheng, and Qianlong Emperors), the Manchu court waged a terrifying ideological war against Han intellectuals to stamp out any lingering anti-Manchu sentiment.

## The Purges
* The court rigorously scrutinized poems, historical texts, and dictionaries for any hidden insults, seditious language, or nostalgia for the fallen Ming Dynasty.
* If a scholar was found guilty, the punishment was absolute: the scholar was executed (often by the brutal "death by a thousand cuts"), their entire extended family was executed or enslaved, and their tombs were desecrated.

## The Chilling Effect
* These inquisitions successfully terrified the Han Chinese intelligentsia into absolute submission and silence. Scholars retreated from political philosophy into safe, obscure textual criticism.
* **Storytelling Hook:** For scholarly Kindred, this era was a nightmare where a single misplaced word in a poem could lead to the destruction of entire mortal bloodlines and the burning of ancient libraries. Han reformist Kindred in 1900 would remember this as the era when the Manchus tried to cut out the tongue of Han culture.
