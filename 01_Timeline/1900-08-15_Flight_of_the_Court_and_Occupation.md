# The Flight of the Court and the Occupation (August 15, 1900 onwards)

As foreign troops swarmed into the capital, the Qing court panicked. 

## The Flight
* **August 15, 1900:** In the early hours of the morning, Empress Dowager Cixi, Emperor Guangxu, and their immediate retinue fled the Forbidden City. 
* Disguised as common peasants in rough clothing, they rode in wooden mule carts out the northern gates, embarking on a grueling "inspection tour" that took them to the western city of Xi'an, far out of reach of the foreign armies.

## The Occupation and Looting
* Beijing was divided into administrative zones controlled by the different foreign powers. 
* What followed was an unprecedented campaign of systematic looting. Priceless artifacts from the Forbidden City, the Summer Palace, temples, and private residences were stolen by foreign soldiers, diplomats, and missionaries. 
* **Punitive Expeditions:** Under the command of German Field Marshal Alfred von Waldersee (who arrived in September), brutal punitive expeditions scoured the countryside to hunt down suspected Boxers, resulting in widespread atrocities and the execution of thousands of innocent civilians.
