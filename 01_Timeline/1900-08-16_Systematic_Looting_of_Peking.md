# Systematic Looting of Peking (August 16, 1900 onwards)

Following the relief of the Legation Quarter and the flight of the Qing Court on August 15, discipline among the Eight-Nation Alliance armies largely broke down as they realized the incredible wealth of the city was completely unguarded.

## The Plunder
* Peking was divided into occupation zones, and the armies engaged in an unprecedented frenzy of looting.
* The Forbidden City, the Summer Palace, ancient temples, and the estates of wealthy Manchu nobles were stripped bare. 
* Soldiers, officers, and even foreign diplomats and missionaries participated. They stole priceless jade carvings, centuries-old silk scrolls, gold bullion, and ancient bronzes.

## The Aftermath
* Much of this stolen wealth was shipped back to Europe, Japan, and the United States, where it filled museums and private collections. 
* In the streets of Peking, makeshift "loot markets" sprang up where soldiers sold stolen imperial treasures for fractions of their true value. The looting completely gutted the cultural and financial heritage of the Qing capital.
