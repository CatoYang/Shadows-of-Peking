# The First Sino-Japanese War (1894 - 1895)

The First Sino-Japanese War was fought between the Qing Dynasty and the Empire of Japan, primarily over influence in Korea. After more than six months of unbroken successes by Japanese land and naval forces and the loss of the Chinese port of Weihaiwei, the Qing government sued for peace in February 1895.

## Key Outcomes
* **Military Devastation:** The modern Beiyang Fleet, pride of the Qing navy, was completely destroyed. The inadequacy of the Qing military's modernization efforts (the Self-Strengthening Movement) was laid bare.
* **Treaty of Shimonoseki:** Signed in April 1895, the treaty forced China to cede Taiwan, the Penghu Islands, and the Liaodong Peninsula to Japan. China also had to pay a massive indemnity of 200 million kuping taels.
* **Psychological Impact:** The defeat by a former tributary state sent shockwaves through Chinese society, sparking national humiliation and fueling both reformist movements and xenophobic anger that would eventually culminate in the Boxer Rebellion.
