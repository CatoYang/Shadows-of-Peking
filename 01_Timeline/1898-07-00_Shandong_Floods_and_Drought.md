# Shandong Floods and Drought (Summer 1898)

While the political crisis unfolded in Beijing, an ecological and humanitarian disaster struck Shandong province, the birthplace of the Boxer movement.

## The Disasters
* **The Yellow River Floods:** The Yellow River, known as "China's Sorrow," burst its banks in summer 1898, inundating massive swaths of farmland and destroying hundreds of villages.
* **The Drought:** The floods were immediately followed by a severe drought in 1899, leading to widespread crop failures and mass starvation. 

## The Blame
* The desperate, starving peasantry turned to superstition and xenophobia to explain the catastrophes. 
* It was widely believed that foreign missionaries and Chinese Christians had angered the local spirits and dragons. The construction of foreign railways and churches with tall spires were accused of severing the "dragon veins" (Feng Shui), causing the heavens to withhold rain.
* This desperation provided the perfect recruiting ground for the nascent Boxer militias.
