# Oboi Regency and Purges

**Date:** 1661–1669

## Historical Context
Following the death of the Shunzhi Emperor, the young Kangxi Emperor ascended to the throne. Real power, however, lay with the Four Regents, the most dominant being the formidable Manchu military commander Oboi. Oboi enacted authoritarian policies, fiercely protecting Manchu privilege and violently purging any political rivals, until the young Kangxi Emperor famously orchestrated a coup to arrest him in 1669.

## Kindred Implications
The Oboi regency marked the first major internal conflict among the Manchu Kindred after their successful conquest of China. The elders who had orchestrated the Vaulderie Pact sought to solidify their absolute control over the Peking Praxis. They used Oboi's mortal purges as a smokescreen to eliminate ambitious neonates within their own ranks who had grown too powerful during the war. This period cemented the rigid, authoritarian structure of the Peking Kindred Court, crushing any early hopes of a decentralized domain.
