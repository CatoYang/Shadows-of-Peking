# Germany Seizes Jiaozhou Bay (November 14, 1897)

Following the murder of two German Catholic priests in the Juye Incident (November 1), Kaiser Wilhelm II seized the opportunity he had long been waiting for to establish a permanent naval foothold in East Asia.

## The Occupation
* On November 14, 1897, the German East Asia Squadron, commanded by Admiral von Diederichs, sailed into Jiaozhou Bay (Kiautschou) on the southern coast of Shandong province.
* They landed marines and captured the Qing fortifications without facing any organized resistance, as the local Qing commander had been ordered not to fire on foreigners to avoid starting a war.

## The Treaty
* Germany forced the Qing government to sign a humiliating treaty, granting Germany a 99-year lease on the bay and the surrounding territory of Qingdao (Tsingtao).
* They also secured exclusive rights to build railways and open coal mines throughout Shandong province.

## Impact
* This aggressive seizure directly triggered the "Scramble for Concessions," where other European powers rushed to claim their own pieces of China. 
* More importantly, the aggressive German railway and mining operations in Shandong severely disrupted local agriculture and traditional Feng Shui, deeply angering the local populace and providing the direct spark for the rise of the Boxer movement in that specific province.
