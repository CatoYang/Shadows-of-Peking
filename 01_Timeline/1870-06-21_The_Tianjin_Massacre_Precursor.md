# The Tianjin Massacre (June 21, 1870)

Though it occurred thirty years before the Boxer Rebellion, the Tianjin Massacre (or Tianjin Incident) established the deeply rooted template for anti-Christian violence and rumors in Northern China.

## The Rumors
* French Catholic nuns operated an orphanage in Tianjin. Because they offered small bounties for orphans brought to them (to save them from infanticide or starvation), rumors spread that they were buying children to kidnap them.
* Epidemics frequently swept through the orphanage. The high death rate, combined with the secretive nature of the Catholic last rites, birthed a widespread rumor that the nuns were murdering children to extract their eyes and internal organs to make foreign medicine and silver.

## The Riot
* A massive, enraged Chinese mob gathered outside the orphanage and the French consulate. 
* When the arrogant French consul fired his pistol into the crowd, the mob rioted. They murdered the consul, several French priests, and 10 nuns, and burned the consulate and cathedral to the ground.
* **Legacy:** The Qing government was forced to pay a massive indemnity and execute the rioters. The rumors of "organ harvesting" and kidnapping by Christians never died, heavily fueling the paranoia of the later Boxer movement.
