# Battle of Tianjin (July 13 - 14, 1900)

Before the foreign powers could march on Beijing to relieve the siege, they had to secure the walled city of Tianjin, which was heavily defended by Qing Imperial troops and Boxers.

## The Battle
* Following the relief of the Seymour Expedition in late June, Alliance forces built up their numbers in the foreign concessions of Tianjin.
* **July 13:** A massive allied assault (roughly 6,000 troops) was launched against the walled Chinese city. The fighting was incredibly bloody, particularly for the Japanese and American contingents (the US 9th Infantry suffered heavily).
* **July 14:** The Alliance forces breached the walls and captured the city. Widespread looting by foreign troops immediately followed.

## Strategic Importance
* The capture of Tianjin broke the main line of Qing defense in the region. 
* It provided the Eight-Nation Alliance with a secure, heavily fortified staging area to amass the large army necessary to march on the capital.
