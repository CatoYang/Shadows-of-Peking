# The Juye Incident (November 1, 1897)

On the night of November 1, 1897, an armed band of men—likely members of the Big Sword Society, a precursor to the Boxers—broke into a German Catholic mission in Zhangjiazhuang, Juye County, Shandong Province.

## The Event
* **The Murders:** The attackers murdered two German priests, Richard Henle and Franz Xavier Nies, who were staying at the mission. A third priest, Georg Stenz (the intended target, who had angered locals), managed to hide and survive.
* **The Motivations:** The local populace was deeply aggrieved by the aggressive behavior of Catholic missionaries, who frequently intervened in local lawsuits to favor Chinese converts, completely disrupting local justice and social order.

## The Aftermath
* **The Pretext:** The German Empire, which had been seeking a naval base in East Asia, used the murders as a direct pretext for military intervention.
* **Seizure of Jiaozhou:** On November 14, the German East Asia Squadron landed troops and seized Jiaozhou Bay (Kiautschou) on the southern coast of Shandong, forcing the Qing government to grant them a 99-year lease. This sparked a chain reaction among other foreign powers.
