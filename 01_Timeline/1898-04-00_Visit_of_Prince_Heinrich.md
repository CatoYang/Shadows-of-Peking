# Visit of Prince Heinrich of Prussia (April 1898)

Following the German seizure of Jiaozhou Bay, Prince Heinrich of Prussia (brother of Kaiser Wilhelm II) made an unprecedented and highly aggressive diplomatic visit to Peking.

## The Diplomatic Breach
* Traditionally, foreign envoys had to jump through immense bureaucratic hoops and adhere to strict court protocol to even glimpse the Emperor. 
* Prince Heinrich demanded—and received, backed by the threat of German naval power—a direct audience with both Emperor Guangxu and Empress Dowager Cixi. 
* He refused to perform the kowtow and insisted on being treated as royalty of equal standing to the Qing Emperor.

## Local Political Impact
* This was viewed as a massive humiliation for the Qing Court. It visually demonstrated to the local populace and the conservative Manchu nobility that the Emperor was powerless against foreign demands. 
* It deeply angered conservative factions, fueling their desire to find a weapon (which would later be the Boxers) to drive the arrogant foreigners out of the capital.
