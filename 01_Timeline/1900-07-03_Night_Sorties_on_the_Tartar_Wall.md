# Night Sorties on the Tartar Wall (July 3, 1900)

The Legation Quarter was pressed directly against the massive, 40-foot-high and 40-foot-wide Tartar Wall. Control of this wall was absolute life or death, as whoever held the high ground could fire directly down into the foreign compounds.

## The Desperate Charge
* Qing troops had constructed massive barricades on top of the wall and were slowly pushing them closer to the American and German positions, preparing to mount artillery that would obliterate the legations.
* In the pitch-black early hours of July 3, American Captain John T. Myers led a desperate, combined sortie of American, British, and Russian marines.
* Sneaking along the top of the massive wall, they launched a surprise bayonet charge against the Chinese barricade. 

## The Brutal Combat
* The combat was terrifying hand-to-hand fighting in the dark. The marines successfully routed the Chinese troops and captured the barricade, securing the most vital defensive position of the entire 55-day siege.
