# The Siege of the Beitang Begins (June 15, 1900)

While the main siege of the Legation Quarter gets the most historical attention, a parallel, much more desperate siege occurred just two miles away at the Beitang (the Northern Cathedral).

## The Situation
* Over 3,000 Chinese Catholic converts, mostly women, children, and the elderly, fled into the cathedral grounds to escape the Boxer massacres in the streets.
* They were defended by Bishop Pierre-Marie Alphonse Favier, a handful of priests, and a tiny contingent of just 43 French and Italian marines.

## The Siege
* The Beitang was entirely cut off from the Legation Quarter. The Boxers and Imperial troops subjected the cathedral to relentless artillery fire, sniper fire, and terrifying mine attacks (tunneling under the walls to plant explosives).
* The defenders suffered horrific casualties and were reduced to eating roots, bark, and stray dogs. The defense of the Beitang is considered one of the most astonishing tactical stands of the entire conflict.
