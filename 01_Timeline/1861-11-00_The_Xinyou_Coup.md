# The Xinyou Coup (November 1861)

The event that birthed Empress Dowager Cixi's absolute dominance over the Qing Dynasty. Following the trauma of the Second Opium War and the death of her husband, the Xianfeng Emperor, in exile, a vicious power struggle ensued.

## The Eight Regents
* Before dying, the Xianfeng Emperor appointed a council of eight senior conservative ministers (led by Sushun) to act as regents for his five-year-old son, the new Tongzhi Emperor.
* The late emperor's two consorts—Empress Dowager Ci'an (the principal wife) and Empress Dowager Cixi (the biological mother of the boy)—were deeply alienated and threatened by these regents.

## The Coup
* Cixi, demonstrating her ruthless political genius for the first time, formed a secret alliance with the late Emperor's brother, Prince Gong, who had been left in Peking to negotiate with the foreigners.
* As the imperial funeral procession returned to Peking, Cixi and Prince Gong launched a swift, bloodless coup. The eight regents were arrested; Sushun was publicly beheaded at the Caishikou Execution Grounds, and others were forced to commit suicide.

## Ruling Behind the Curtains
* Following the coup, Cixi and Ci'an established the precedent of *Chuilian Tingzheng* ("Ruling from behind the curtains"). As women were forbidden from the throne, they literally sat behind a silk screen during audiences, whispering orders to the child emperor. 
* **Storytelling Hook:** This is when Cixi first seized the reins of the empire. For Elder Kindred who observe mortal politics, the Xinyou Coup was a masterclass in shifting power dynamics, showing that the true ruler of China was no longer the Emperor, but the woman behind the screen.
