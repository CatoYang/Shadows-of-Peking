# Taiping Capture of Nanjing

**Date:** March 19, 1853

## Historical Context
The Taiping Rebellion, led by Hong Xiuquan (who claimed to be the younger brother of Jesus Christ), captured the major southern city of Nanjing. They slaughtered the entire Manchu population of the city and renamed it Tianjing (Heavenly Capital). From this base, the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom waged a massive, devastating civil war against the Qing dynasty that would last for over a decade and cost tens of millions of lives.

## Kindred Implications
The capture of Nanjing was a monumental victory for the Christian Kindred (primarily manipulating the rebellion from the shadows). They established a massive stronghold in the south, actively warring against both the Manchu "Conquerors" of Peking and the ancient Han vampires ("The Subjugated") who had long claimed the south as their refuge. The slaughter of the Manchu garrison included the destruction of any Manchu vampires stationed there. This event severely crippled the Qing Kindred Court's influence south of the Yangtze River and planted the seeds for the intense hatred of Christian Kindred ("Er Maozi") seen during the Boxer Rebellion.
