# The Watergate Breakthrough (August 14, 1900)

After 55 agonizing days of siege, the relief of the Legation Quarter finally arrived, but the breach came from beneath the city walls rather than over them.

## The Assault on Peking
* On August 14, the Eight-Nation Alliance launched a massive, uncoordinated assault on the outer walls of Peking. The armies essentially raced each other for the glory of being the first to relieve the siege.
* The Japanese and Russians became bogged down in heavy, bloody fighting trying to batter down the main reinforced gates. The Americans engaged in fierce combat scaling the 40-foot Tartar Wall.

## The Sluice Gate
* The British contingent (largely comprised of seasoned Indian Rajput and Sikh regiments under General Alfred Gaselee) took a different approach.
* Following a secret map or the guidance of a smuggled Chinese Christian messenger, the British-Indian troops located a dried-out "watergate"—a heavy iron sluice grate used for sewage and drainage that ran directly underneath the massive Tartar Wall and into the Legation Quarter.

## The Relief
* The troops smashed through the rusted iron bars and waded through the muck and mud beneath the wall. 
* Because the Qing defenders were entirely focused on the walls and main gates above, this subterranean route was practically unguarded. 
* The Sikh and Rajput troops emerged blinking into the sunlight inside the Legation Quarter, becoming the very first allied soldiers to break the siege, greeted by the weeping and cheering survivors.
* **Storytelling Hook:** A secret, subterranean sewage entrance into the most heavily fortified part of the city? This screams Nosferatu involvement, or a crucial path for Kindred trying to sneak in or out of the city during the height of the battle without facing the sunlight or the armies above.
