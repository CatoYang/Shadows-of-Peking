# The Battle of Langfang (June 18, 1900)

The Seymour Expedition (the initial foreign rescue force) did not just peacefully decide to turn back; they were brutally defeated in a battle that shocked the world.

## The Ambush
* As Admiral Seymour’s 2,000-man multinational force tried to repair the railway tracks near Langfang (halfway between Tianjin and Peking), they were ambushed by an overwhelming force.
* This wasn't just Boxers armed with swords. It was a highly coordinated attack combining thousands of Boxers with the modernized Qing Imperial troops of General Nie Shicheng, who were armed with modern Mauser rifles and artillery.

## The Turning Point
* The foreign troops, who heavily underestimated Chinese military capabilities, were pinned down and suffered hundreds of casualties. Running out of ammunition and completely surrounded, Seymour was forced to order a humiliating retreat back to Tianjin.
* **Political Impact:** This victory elated the pro-Boxer faction in the Imperial Court. It seemingly proved that the Boxers, when supported by the regular army, could actually defeat modern Western militaries, pushing Empress Dowager Cixi confidently toward a declaration of war.
