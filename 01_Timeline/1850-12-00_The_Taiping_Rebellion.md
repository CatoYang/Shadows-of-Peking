# The Taiping Rebellion (1850 - 1864)

The most devastating civil war in human history, fueled primarily by Han Chinese resentment against the Manchu rulers and exacerbated by foreign imperialism.

## The Heavenly Kingdom
* Led by Hong Xiuquan (a Hakka Han Chinese man who believed he was the younger brother of Jesus Christ), the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom rose up in southern China.
* Their ideology combined a radical, distorted form of Christianity with intense anti-Manchu nationalism. They explicitly viewed the Manchus as literal "demons" that needed to be exterminated.

## The Carnage
* The rebellion consumed southern and central China for 14 years. It is estimated that between 20 and 30 million people died from warfare, famine, and plague.
* The Qing military was so weak that the Manchu court had to rely on Han Chinese provincial generals (like Zeng Guofan) and foreign mercenary armies to finally crush the rebellion.
* **The Impact:** The rebellion permanently broke the central power of the Manchu court, shifting massive military and economic power into the hands of Han provincial governors (like Li Hongzhang and Yuan Shikai), perfectly setting the stage for the Han/Manchu factional split during the Boxer Rebellion.
