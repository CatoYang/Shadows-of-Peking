# Tan Sitong's Secret Visit to Yuan Shikai (September 18, 1898)

Knowing that Cixi was preparing to use Ronglu's troops to end the reforms, the radical reformers launched a desperate, eleventh-hour plot to stage a preemptive military coup.

## The Late-Night Meeting
* On the night of September 18, the brilliant scholar and martial artist Tan Sitong sneaked into the Peking residence of General Yuan Shikai (who commanded the modernized Beiyang New Army under Ronglu).
* Tan Sitong produced a secret document—allegedly a blood-written edict from Emperor Guangxu, though historians debate if the reformers forged or exaggerated it—ordering Yuan to act.
* **The Request:** Tan flatly asked Yuan Shikai to immediately travel to Tianjin, assassinate his commanding officer (Ronglu), take total command of the northern armies, and march on the Summer Palace to place Empress Dowager Cixi under armed house arrest (or kill her).

## The Betrayal
* Swearing loyalty to the Emperor's cause to Tan Sitong's face, Yuan agreed to the plot.
* However, Yuan was a ruthless pragmatist. He knew his troops were outnumbered by Ronglu's other loyalist forces, and he correctly calculated that the Emperor's faction was doomed. 
* On September 20, Yuan took the train to Tianjin and went straight to Ronglu, exposing the entire assassination plot. Ronglu immediately took a special train to Peking, and Cixi launched the Wuxu Coup the very next morning, leading to Tan Sitong's execution.
