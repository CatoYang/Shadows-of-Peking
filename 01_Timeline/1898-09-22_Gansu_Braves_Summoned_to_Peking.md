# Gansu Braves Summoned to Peking (Autumn 1898)

In the tense atmosphere surrounding the Hundred Days' Reform and the subsequent Wuxu Coup, Empress Dowager Cixi and her top military commander, Ronglu, took steps to heavily militarize the capital to prevent any further reformist plots or foreign intervention.

## The Summons
* General Dong Fuxiang was ordered to bring his fearsome Kansu Braves (Gansu Army)—roughly 10,000 battle-hardened, predominantly Muslim troops—from the remote western frontier to the Peking area.
* They were incorporated into the newly formed "Wuwei Corps" (Guards Army), serving as the Rear Division.

## Escalation of Tensions
* The arrival of the Kansu Braves immediately escalated tensions in the capital. Unlike the disciplined, modernized troops of Yuan Shikai or the complacent Manchu Bannermen, the Kansu Braves were fanatically traditionalist and deeply xenophobic.
* **Clashes:** Almost immediately upon arrival in late 1898, the Kansu Braves began clashing with foreigners. At the Marco Polo Bridge outside Peking, they harassed and attacked foreign railway engineers and diplomats.
* The foreign ministers were so terrified of the Kansu Braves that they successfully demanded the Qing court move them further outside the city walls. However, the troops remained nearby, forming a hostile, volatile ring around the capital that would later eagerly participate in the siege of the Legations.
