# The Seymour Expedition (June 1900)

As thousands of Boxers converged on Beijing, the foreign diplomats in the Legation Quarter realized they were in mortal danger and telegraphed for immediate military rescue.

## The Expedition
* **June 9, 1900:** A hastily assembled multinational force of roughly 2,000 sailors and marines, commanded by British Vice-Admiral Edward Seymour, left Tianjin by train to relieve the Beijing legations.
* **The Blockade:** The expedition's progress was agonizingly slow. Boxers and sympathetic Imperial troops had torn up the railway tracks ahead of them. 
* By June 18, facing overwhelming numbers of Boxers and regular Qing troops under General Nie Shicheng at Langfang, and running dangerously low on ammunition and supplies, Seymour was forced to order a retreat.
* **June 26, 1900:** The expedition limped back into Tianjin, having suffered over 300 casualties. The failure of the Seymour Expedition shocked the foreign powers and emboldened the Qing court.
