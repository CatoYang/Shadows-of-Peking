# The Liyuan Clashes and Church Plundering (October 1898)

As the drought worsened in Shandong, the nascent Boxer groups (then known as the Plum Blossom Fists or Righteous and Harmonious Fists) began taking direct, organized action against Christians.

## The Extortion Campaign
* In late 1898, Boxer bands around Liyuan (Shandong province) began marching on the homes of wealthy Chinese Christians. 
* They did not initially massacre them; instead, they demanded "fines" or "donations" of grain and silver to compensate the village for the taxes the Christians had refused to pay for temple festivals.
* If the Christians refused, the Boxers would loot their homes, slaughter their livestock, and burn their chapels.

## The Escalation
* Local magistrates, sympathetic to the starving non-Christian peasants and annoyed by the arrogant missionaries, often turned a blind eye to this extortion. 
* This lack of government intervention emboldened the Boxers, teaching them that they could attack Christians with impunity. It served as the crucial stepping stone from localized extortion to full-blown, murderous rebellion.
