# The Grand Council Debate (June 8, 1900)

As the Boxers encircled and began infiltrating Peking, Empress Dowager Cixi convened the Grand Council to decide the fate of the Empire. The court was bitterly divided.

## The Factions
* **The Pro-Boxer Faction:** Led by Prince Duan and Prince Zhuang, they argued that the Boxers' "spirit magic" was real, that they were loyal patriots, and that the court should arm them to drive the foreigners into the sea.
* **The Moderate Faction:** Led by officials like Ronglu (commander of the modern Imperial armies), Xu Jingcheng, and Yuan Chang. They argued that relying on superstitious peasant militias was madness, that foreign armies would crush them, and that attacking the diplomats violated all rules of statecraft and would lead to the partition of China.

## The Outcome
* Cixi hesitated, caught between her fear of foreign partition and her fear of a domestic rebellion if she crushed the Boxers. She ultimately adopted a two-faced policy: issuing edicts to officially suppress the Boxers while secretly sending them grain and allowing them to roam the capital.
