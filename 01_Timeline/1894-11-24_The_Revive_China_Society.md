# The Revive China Society (November 24, 1894)

As the Qing Dynasty suffered a humiliating defeat against Japan in 1894, modern Han Chinese nationalism began to transition from secret societies into organized, modern revolutionary movements.

## Sun Yat-sen and the Xingzhonghui
* Founded by the Western-educated doctor Sun Yat-sen in Honolulu, Hawaii, the Revive China Society (*Xingzhonghui*) was one of the first explicitly republican revolutionary groups.
* Their foundational oath was: *"Expel the Tatar barbarians (Manchus), revive Zhonghua (China), and establish a unified government."*

## The Ideological Shift
* Unlike the Boxers, who were fiercely anti-foreign but ultimately supported the Qing, the Revive China Society believed the Manchu dynasty itself was the absolute root of China's weakness and had to be violently overthrown to save the nation.
* **Storytelling Hook:** This society marks the beginning of the end for the Qing. Anarch/Reformist Kindred would heavily patronize or infiltrate groups like this. While the Manchu court is distracted by the Boxers and the foreigners in 1900, these modern revolutionaries are quietly preparing to burn the entire empire down.
