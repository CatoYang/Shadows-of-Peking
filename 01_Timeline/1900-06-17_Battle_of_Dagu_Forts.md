# Battle of the Dagu Forts (June 17, 1900)

With the Seymour Expedition trapped and Beijing cut off, the naval commanders of the Eight-Nation Alliance anchored off the coast realized they needed to secure their supply line up the Hai River to Tianjin.

## The Ultimatum and Battle
* **June 16:** The Alliance commanders issued an ultimatum demanding the surrender of the heavily fortified Dagu (Taku) Forts at the mouth of the river. 
* The Qing commander, General Luo Rongguang, refused.
* **June 17:** The forts opened fire on the foreign gunboats. A fierce naval bombardment ensued, followed by a land assault by Alliance marines. 
* The Alliance captured the forts, suffering moderate casualties, while inflicting heavy losses on the Qing defenders.

## The Consequence
* The capture of the Dagu Forts was a major escalation. When news reached Beijing, Empress Dowager Cixi viewed it as an unprovoked act of war by the foreign powers, tipping the divided court fully in favor of supporting the Boxers and going to war.
