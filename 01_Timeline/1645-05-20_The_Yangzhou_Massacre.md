# The Yangzhou Massacre (May 1645)

As the Manchus swept south to conquer the remnants of the Ming Dynasty, they encountered fierce resistance from Han Chinese loyalists, leading to one of the most horrific atrocities in Chinese history.

## The Ten Days of Yangzhou
* The wealthy, highly cultured city of Yangzhou fiercely resisted the Manchu siege under the command of Han general Shi Kefa.
* When the city walls finally fell on May 20, 1645, the Manchu commander Prince Dodo ordered a punitive massacre to terrorize southern China into submission.
* For ten continuous days, the Manchu troops indiscriminately slaughtered the Han Chinese population. Historical accounts claim over 800,000 civilians were murdered, though modern historians debate the exact number.

## The Impact
* The Yangzhou Massacre left a permanent, unhealing scar on the Han Chinese psyche. Anti-Qing secret societies used the memory of Yangzhou as a rallying cry for centuries.
* **Storytelling Hook:** The sheer volume of blood spilled in Yangzhou makes it a dark legend among Kindred. Han Elder vampires who survived the massacre would harbor an ancient, burning hatred for the Manchu court and any Manchu Kindred.
