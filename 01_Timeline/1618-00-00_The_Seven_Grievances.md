# The Seven Grievances (1618)

The ultimate root of the Manchu-Han conflict began before the Qing Dynasty even existed, when the Manchus (then known as the Jurchens) declared war on the Han-ruled Ming Dynasty.

## The Declaration
* In 1618, the Jurchen chieftain Nurhaci (who would posthumously become the founding emperor of the Qing) authored a manifesto known as the *Seven Grievances* (Qida Hen).
* The document listed his deep-seated resentments against the Ming Dynasty, accusing them of murdering his father and grandfather, violating borders, and showing extreme bias against the Jurchen people.

## The Impact
* This manifesto served as the formal declaration of war and the ideological justification for the brutal, decades-long conquest of China.
* **Storytelling Hook:** For Elder Kindred who have been active for centuries, the Seven Grievances marks the absolute beginning of the blood feud. Manchu vampires might still quote it to justify their rule over the Han, while Han Elders see it as the document that doomed their golden age.
