# The Arrow Incident (October 8, 1856)

The spark that ignited the devastating Second Opium War, escalating foreign military intervention in China to an unprecedented level.

## The Incident
* The *Arrow* was a Chinese-owned lorcha (a small sailing vessel) that was registered in Hong Kong and flew the British flag.
* On October 8, 1856, Qing marines boarded the *Arrow* in Canton, suspecting the crew of piracy and smuggling. They arrested twelve Chinese crew members and allegedly tore down the British flag.

## The Pretext for War
* The British Consul in Canton, Harry Parkes, seized upon this relatively minor incident as a pretext to launch a massive military response. 
* Claiming an insult to the British flag, the British Navy bombarded Canton. The French soon joined the war, using the execution of a French missionary (Auguste Chapdelaine) as their own pretext.
* **Storytelling Hook:** This event perfectly demonstrates the arrogant, trigger-happy nature of "gunboat diplomacy." Foreign Kindred in the treaty ports might have actually orchestrated the tearing down of the flag just to provoke a war and expand their hunting grounds northward.
