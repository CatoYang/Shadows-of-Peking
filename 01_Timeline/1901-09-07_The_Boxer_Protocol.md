# The Boxer Protocol (September 7, 1901)

After a year of occupation, military expeditions, and protracted negotiations, the Qing Empire was forced to sign the Boxer Protocol (or the Peace Agreement between the Great Powers and China). 

## Punishing Terms
The treaty was heavily unequal and deeply humiliating for China:
* **The Indemnity:** China was forced to pay 450 million taels of silver (approximately $333 million at the time) to the eight nations, to be paid over 39 years with interest, eventually totaling nearly 1 billion taels. This was more than the government's total annual tax revenue.
* **Executions:** Ten high-ranking Qing officials who had supported the Boxers were executed, and others were exiled.
* **Foreign Garrisons:** The foreign powers were granted the right to permanently station troops in Beijing to guard the Legation Quarter, which was expanded and heavily fortified.
* **Fortifications:** The Dagu Forts and other coastal defenses between Beijing and the sea were ordered to be completely dismantled.

## The Aftermath
* The treaty allowed Empress Dowager Cixi to remain in power to ensure the indemnity was paid. She and the Emperor returned to Beijing via train in January 1902.
* The catastrophic failure of the Boxer Rebellion and the crushing weight of the Protocol destroyed whatever lingering legitimacy the Qing Dynasty had left, paving the way for the Xinhai Revolution of 1911.
