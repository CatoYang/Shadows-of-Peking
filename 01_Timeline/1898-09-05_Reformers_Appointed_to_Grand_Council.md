# Reformers Appointed to the Grand Council (September 5, 1898)

Realizing that his radical edicts were being deliberately delayed, ignored, or sabotaged by the conservative senior ministers of the Grand Council and Zongli Yamen, Emperor Guangxu took drastic action to bypass them.

## The Appointments
* Guangxu appointed four of the most radical, junior Han Chinese reformers—Tan Sitong, Lin Xu, Yang Rui, and Liu Guangdi (four of the soon-to-be "Six Gentlemen")—as special secretaries to the Grand Council.
* He granted them unprecedented authority to draft imperial edicts directly and read memorials (petitions) before the senior ministers could filter them.

## The Escalation
* This move completely circumvented the traditional chain of command. The senior Manchu ministers were humiliated and stripped of their gatekeeping power. The political temperature in the Forbidden City reached a boiling point, as it became clear the Emperor was dismantling the conservative power structure from the inside.
