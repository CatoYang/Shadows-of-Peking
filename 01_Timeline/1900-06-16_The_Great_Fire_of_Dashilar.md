# The Great Fire of Dashilar (June 16, 1900)

As the Boxers established control over the streets of Peking, their zeal to destroy anything foreign led to an unprecedented civilian catastrophe that devastated the city's economy.

## The Fire
* Dashilar was the incredibly wealthy, densely populated commercial and entertainment district located just outside the Qianmen Gate (the main southern gate of the Tartar Wall).
* On June 16, a group of Boxers targeted the "Lao Desheng" pharmacy because it sold foreign-made medicines. They set it on fire.
* The fire quickly raged out of control, spreading through the tightly packed wooden structures of the district.

## The Devastation
* The inferno destroyed over 4,000 shops, banks, theaters, and private homes. 
* It was a massive economic blow to the Han Chinese elite and merchant classes of Peking. Countless lives and fortunes were wiped out in a single night, and the sky over the city was choked with thick, black smoke for days, heightening the apocalyptic atmosphere leading into the siege.
