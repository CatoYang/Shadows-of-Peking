# The Rise of the Boxers (1899)

Out of the desperation in Shandong emerged the *Yihetuan*, or the "Righteous and Harmonious Fists" (dubbed "Boxers" by Westerners due to their martial arts practices).

## The Practices
* **Spirit Possession:** The Boxers practiced martial arts combined with esoteric rituals, incantations, and spirit possession. They believed these rituals rendered them invulnerable to foreign bullets and swords.
* **Demographics:** The movement was largely composed of displaced peasants, unemployed transport workers (due to the railways), and impoverished youths.

## The Slogan Shift
* Initially, many secret societies in the region were anti-Qing ("Overthrow the Qing, Destroy the Foreign").
* By late 1899, recognizing a common enemy, the movement adopted a new rallying cry: **"Support the Qing, Destroy the Foreigners"** (Fu Qing Mie Yang). 
* This crucial shift allowed sympathetic conservative Qing officials to view the Boxers as a loyal, patriotic militia rather than a rebel threat, protecting them from suppression.

## Escalation
* **December 31, 1899:** Boxers murdered S.M. Brooks, a British Anglican missionary, sparking international outrage and demands for the Qing government to crush the movement.
