# The Gaselee Expedition and the Relief of Peking (August 1900)

With Tianjin secured, the Eight-Nation Alliance amassed a force of approximately 20,000 troops, commanded by British General Alfred Gaselee. The force was predominantly Japanese, Russian, British (including Indian regiments), and American.

## The March
* **August 4, 1900:** The expedition departed Tianjin, following the course of the Peiho (Hai) River.
* They faced and defeated Qing Imperial forces at the **Battle of Beicang (August 5)** and the **Battle of Yangcun (August 6)**. Following these defeats, Qing resistance largely collapsed as soldiers deserted.

## The Relief of the Legations
* **August 14, 1900:** The Alliance reached the walls of Beijing. A chaotic race ensued among the national contingents to be the first to breach the city.
* American troops scaled the outer Tartar Wall, while Russian and Japanese forces assaulted the city gates. 
* British-Indian troops (Rajput and Sikh regiments) found an unguarded water gate and were the first to enter the Legation Quarter, effectively ending the 55-day siege.
