# Fall of Peking and the Southern Ming

**Date:** April 25, 1644

## Historical Context
The peasant rebel leader Li Zicheng breached the gates of Peking. In despair, the Chongzhen Emperor hanged himself on a tree in Jingshan Park, officially ending the Ming dynasty in the north. Shortly after, the Manchu armies crossed the Great Wall at Shanhai Pass and defeated Li Zicheng, taking control of Peking. In response, Ming loyalists fled south to Nanjing, establishing the Southern Ming dynasty in an attempt to resist the Qing conquest.

## Kindred Implications
The fall of Peking was a bloodbath for both mortals and Kindred. The Manchu vampires, riding with the Qing armies, systematically hunted down and destroyed the Han Kindred of the capital who had ruled during the Ming era. Many of the oldest Han elders, realizing the city was lost, fled south alongside the mortal Ming loyalists to Nanjing. This mass exodus established Nanjing (and the south generally) as a stronghold for the surviving "Subjugated" Han vampires, harboring deep resentment against the Manchu "Conquerors."
