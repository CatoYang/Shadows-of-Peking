# Dismissal of Weng Tonghe (June 15, 1898)

Just four days after Emperor Guangxu launched the Hundred Days' Reform, Empress Dowager Cixi struck her first major blow against the reformist movement, signaling the intense factional war to come.

## The Dismissal
* Weng Tonghe was an immensely respected conservative scholar, the Imperial Tutor to Emperor Guangxu, and a critical moderating figure. 
* Though somewhat conservative, Weng had introduced Kang Youwei to the Emperor, facilitating the start of the reforms.
* Cixi recognized Weng's profound influence over the Emperor. She forced Guangxu to issue an edict abruptly stripping Weng of all his ranks and offices and banishing him to his home province.

## Political Impact
* This was a massive warning shot. By removing the Emperor's most trusted advisor, Cixi isolated Guangxu and demonstrated that while he could issue edicts, she still controlled the ultimate levers of personnel and power within the court.
* It forced the radical reformers (like Kang Youwei) to operate without moderate political cover, accelerating the polarization between the Emperor and the Empress Dowager.
