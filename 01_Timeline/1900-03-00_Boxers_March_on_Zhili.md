# The Boxers March on Zhili (Spring 1900)

Under immense foreign pressure, the Qing court appointed the pragmatic and modernized military commander Yuan Shikai as the Governor of Shandong in late 1899.

## The Expulsion from Shandong
* Yuan Shikai ruthlessly suppressed the Boxers in Shandong, executing their leaders and breaking their power in the province.
* However, rather than destroying the movement entirely, this merely drove the surviving Boxers north into Zhili (the province surrounding Beijing).

## The Imperial Edict
* **January 11, 1900:** Empress Dowager Cixi issued a controversial edict instructing local officials to distinguish between "good" and "bad" secret societies, effectively ordering them not to blindly crush the Boxers. This was interpreted by the Boxers as tacit Imperial approval.
* As the Boxers moved through Zhili towards the capital, their ranks swelled. They systematically tore up railway tracks, cut telegraph lines, and massacred Chinese Christians, severely isolating the capital from the coast.
