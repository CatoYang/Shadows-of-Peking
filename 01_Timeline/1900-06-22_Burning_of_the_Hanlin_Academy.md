# The Burning of the Hanlin Academy (June 22, 1900)

In one of the most culturally devastating acts of the entire Boxer Rebellion, the Qing forces sacrificed the intellectual heart of their own empire in a desperate attempt to kill the foreigners.

## The Hanlin Academy
* The Hanlin Academy was the most prestigious scholarly institution and library in all of China, housing the brightest minds of the empire and thousands of irreplaceable, centuries-old manuscripts. 
* Crucially for the siege, the Academy was located immediately adjacent to the northern wall of the British Legation (the main defensive stronghold of the foreigners).

## The Fire
* On June 22, the anti-foreign Kansu Braves, commanded by General Dong Fuxiang, deliberately set the Hanlin Academy on fire. They hoped the strong northern winds would carry the inferno directly into the British compound and burn the foreigners alive.
* The wind suddenly shifted, saving the British Legation, but the Academy was entirely consumed by flames.

## Cultural Catastrophe
* The fire destroyed countless priceless texts, most notably the only surviving complete copies of the *Yongle Encyclopedia* (a massive, irreplaceable compilation of Chinese history, philosophy, and art commissioned in 1403). 
* Both foreign marines and Chinese scholars desperately tried to drag burning books from the flames, but the vast majority of the archive was lost to ashes. 
* **Storytelling Hook:** For Kindred (especially Tremere or Elder scholars), the burning of the Hanlin is an apocalyptic loss of occult lore, ancient history, and magical secrets. The chaos of the fire is the perfect scene for vampires desperately trying to steal or save forbidden texts.
