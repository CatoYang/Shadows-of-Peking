# The Pingyuan Incident (October 18, 1899)

The Pingyuan Incident was the first major, organized military clash between the Boxers and regular Qing Imperial troops, and a major turning point in the movement's trajectory.

## The Clash
* Following continued looting of Christian homes in Pingyuan County (Shandong), the local magistrate panicked and called for military support.
* A detachment of Qing Imperial troops, armed with modern rifles, engaged a large force of Boxers at the Senluo Temple. 
* Despite their belief in spiritual invulnerability, the Boxers suffered heavy casualties (dozens killed) against the modern firearms, though they fought fiercely enough to force the Qing troops to eventually withdraw.

## The Strategic Shift
* The battle proved to the Boxers that they could not fight both the foreigners *and* the Qing Imperial army simultaneously.
* In the aftermath of Pingyuan, Boxer leaders made the calculated decision to strictly forbid looting from non-Christians and officially adopted the pro-government slogan: **"Support the Qing, Destroy the Foreigners."**
* This shift successfully fractured the Qing government's response, leading Empress Dowager Cixi to view them as misguided patriots rather than treasonous rebels.
