# Convention of Beijing (October 24, 1860)

Following the flight of the Emperor and the fiery destruction of the Old Summer Palace, the Qing Dynasty had no choice but to completely capitulate to the Anglo-French forces.

## The Surrender
* With Emperor Xianfeng hiding in Rehe, his brother, Prince Gong, was left in Peking to negotiate the surrender.
* On October 24, 1860, Prince Gong signed the Convention of Beijing, bringing a definitive end to the Second Opium War.

## The Final Terms
* The convention officially ratified the hated Treaties of Tientsin from 1858, cementing the permanent presence of foreign legations in Peking.
* It ceded the Kowloon Peninsula (opposite Hong Kong island) to the British in perpetuity.
* It demanded a massive indemnity of 8 million taels of silver each to Britain and France.
* **Storytelling Hook:** This is the legal foundation for the Legation Quarter in Peking. By 1900, the foreign presence in the capital wasn't just tolerated; it was an entrenched, heavily defended legal right established by this exact treaty 40 years prior, breeding decades of silent resentment among the Manchu elite.
