# The Scramble for Concessions (1898)

Following Germany's seizure of Jiaozhou Bay in late 1897, the other major imperial powers rushed to carve China into exclusive "spheres of influence," fearing they would be left out of the division of the Qing Empire.

## The Divisions
* **Russia:** Demanded and received a 25-year lease on Port Arthur and the Liaodong Peninsula, giving them a warm-water port and dominance over Manchuria.
* **Great Britain:** Leased the New Territories (expanding Hong Kong) for 99 years and secured Weihaiwei in Shandong to counter the Russian and German presence.
* **France:** Secured a 99-year lease on Guangzhouwan in southern China and expanded their influence from Indochina into Yunnan, Guangxi, and Guangdong provinces.

## Domestic Impact
* The Qing Empire appeared to be on the verge of being "sliced like a melon" by foreign powers.
* Foreign companies gained exclusive rights to build railways and operate mines. The construction of railways frequently destroyed farmland, disturbed ancestral graves, and put traditional transport workers out of business, generating immense local hostility.
