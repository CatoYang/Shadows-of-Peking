# The Hundred Days' Reform Begins (June 11, 1898)

Traumatized by the catastrophic defeat to Japan in 1895 and the subsequent carving up of China by European powers in 1897-1898, the young Emperor Guangxu determined that the Qing Dynasty would collapse if it did not rapidly modernize.

## The Edicts
* Guided by progressive, radical scholars like Kang Youwei and Liang Qichao, the Emperor issued his first reform decree on June 11, 1898.
* Over the next 103 days, Guangxu issued a dizzying flurry of over 40 imperial edicts.
* **The Goals:** The reforms aimed to:
  * Abolish the archaic civil service examination system and replace it with modern educational institutions teaching science and Western political thought.
  * Modernize and Westernize the military.
  * Rapidly industrialize the economy and encourage capitalist enterprise.
  * Eliminate the thousands of corrupt sinecures (do-nothing jobs) held by conservative Manchu nobility.

## The Backlash
* While supported by the Han Chinese intelligentsia, the sheer speed and radical nature of the reforms terrified the entrenched conservative establishment. The Manchu nobility realized these reforms would completely strip them of their generational privileges and power.
