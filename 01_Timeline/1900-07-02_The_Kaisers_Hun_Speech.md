# The Kaiser's "Hun Speech" (July 2, 1900)

Following the assassination of the German Minister von Ketteler, Kaiser Wilhelm II delivered a violently racist and inflammatory speech to departing German troops in Bremerhaven that would define the utter brutality of the postwar occupation.

## The "Yellow Peril"
* Enraged by the murder of his diplomat, the Kaiser ordered his troops to take no prisoners and show no mercy.
* He famously declared: *"Should you encounter the enemy, he will be defeated! No quarter will be given! Prisoners will not be taken! Whoever falls into your hands is forfeited. Just as a thousand years ago the Huns under their King Attila made a name for themselves... so may the name German be affirmed by you in such a way in China that no Chinese will ever again dare to look cross-eyed at a German."*

## The Brutal Reality
* The soldiers of the Eight-Nation Alliance, particularly the Germans under Field Marshal von Waldersee, took this speech literally. 
* It provided the ideological justification for the "punitive expeditions" that scoured the countryside surrounding Peking after the siege, resulting in the mass execution of thousands of innocent Chinese civilians on the mere suspicion of being Boxers.
