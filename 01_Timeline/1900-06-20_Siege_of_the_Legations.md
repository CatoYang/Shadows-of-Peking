# The Siege of the Legation Quarter (June 20 - August 14, 1900)

The conflict reached a boiling point in the heart of Beijing. On June 19, the Qing foreign office (Zongli Yamen) gave all foreign diplomats 24 hours to evacuate the city. Believing it was a trap to slaughter them on the roads, they refused.

## The Assassination
* **June 20, 1900:** The German Minister, Clemens von Ketteler, was shot and killed in his palanquin by a Qing Manchu bannerman named Enhai while en route to the Zongli Yamen to protest the evacuation order.

## The Siege Begins
* Following the murder, Imperial troops (specifically the fierce Muslim Kansu Braves) and Boxers began opening fire on the Legation Quarter. 
* **June 21, 1900:** Empress Dowager Cixi officially issued an Imperial Decree declaring war on all eight foreign powers simultaneously. 
* **The Hanlin Academy:** On June 22, the Kansu Braves deliberately set fire to the Hanlin Academy—which housed the oldest and most precious library in China—hoping the winds would spread the fire to the adjacent British Legation. Countless irreplaceable historical texts were destroyed.
* The siege would last 55 days. Approximately 400 foreign guards, 400 diplomats/civilians, and 3,000 Chinese Christians defended a hastily fortified perimeter against thousands of attackers.
