# The Taiyuan Massacre (July 9, 1900)

While the violence in Peking gets the most attention, the most brutal and concentrated massacre of foreigners occurred in Taiyuan, the capital of Shanxi province.

## Governor Yuxian
* The governor of Shanxi was Yuxian. He had previously been the governor of Shandong, where he heavily patronized and protected the early Boxers before being replaced by Yuan Shikai.
* Fervently anti-foreign, Yuxian invited all the Protestant missionaries, their wives, and children scattered across the province to seek refuge in his gubernatorial compound in Taiyuan, promising them official protection from the roaming Boxers.

## The Execution
* It was a trap. On July 9, Yuxian summoned the missionaries to his courtyard. 
* He ordered his troops to strip them to the waist, and then personally supervised as his soldiers hacked them to death with swords. 
* 44 foreigners (including women and children) and dozens of Chinese Christians were beheaded in a single afternoon. 
* **Impact:** This event made Yuxian the most wanted man by the foreign powers. Following the Boxer Protocol, the Qing Court was forced to execute Yuxian to appease the allies.
