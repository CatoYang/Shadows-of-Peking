# The Treaty of Shimonoseki (April 17, 1895)

While the Unequal Treaties began in the 1840s with Western powers, the Treaty of Shimonoseki in 1895 (which ended the First Sino-Japanese War) was uniquely devastating and acted as the direct catalyst for the crises leading to the Boxer Rebellion.

## The Shock
* Losing to Western empires was humiliating, but losing to Japan—a former tributary state that the Chinese historically viewed as culturally inferior—was a massive psychological blow to the Qing Dynasty. It proved the total failure of China's "Self-Strengthening" modernization efforts.

## The Punishing Terms
* **Territorial Loss:** China was forced to cede the island of Taiwan, the Penghu Islands, and the Liaodong Peninsula to Japan in perpetuity. (The European powers later intervened to force Japan to give back Liaodong, only so Russia could take it for themselves later).
* **Korean Independence:** China had to completely renounce its suzerainty over Korea, allowing Japan to turn it into a puppet state.
* **The Indemnity:** China was forced to pay a staggering indemnity of 200 million kuping taels of silver (more than three times the Qing government's annual revenue). To pay this, the Qing had to take out massive, predatory loans from European banks, essentially mortgaging the country's future tax revenues to foreigners.
* **Manufacturing Rights:** Crucially, Japan won the right to operate factories and manufacturing industries inside Chinese treaty ports. Due to the "Most Favored Nation" clauses in previous treaties, all European powers immediately gained this right as well, devastating traditional Chinese cottage industries.
