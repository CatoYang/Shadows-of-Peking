# Subterranean Mine Warfare at the Beitang (July - August 1900)

While the Legation Quarter faced artillery and snipers, the defenders of the Beitang (Northern Cathedral)—which included 3,000 Chinese Christians and 43 French/Italian marines—faced a horrific subterranean threat.

## The Tunnels
* Unable to breach the cathedral's thick perimeter walls with infantry assaults, the Qing forces brought in professional coal miners from the western hills.
* These miners dug deep tunnels beneath the cathedral grounds, packing them with massive quantities of gunpowder.

## The Explosions
* Throughout July and August, several of these mines were detonated. The explosions were catastrophic, creating massive craters that swallowed entire buildings. 
* The largest explosion occurred on August 12, completely destroying a building housing children and killing over 400 Chinese Christians in an instant. 
* This created an atmosphere of absolute, paranoid terror among the defenders, who constantly listened for the sounds of digging beneath their feet.
