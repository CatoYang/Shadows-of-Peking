# Abolition of Sinecures (August 30, 1898)

As the Hundred Days' Reform accelerated into late summer, the Emperor's edicts shifted from educational reforms to attacking the deeply entrenched corruption of the Qing government structure.

## The Purge of Posts
* Guangxu issued sweeping edicts abolishing several central government bureaus and thousands of provincial sinecures—redundant, "do-nothing" government jobs that existed purely to pay salaries to the nobility.
* He also ordered deep cuts to the traditional stipends paid to Manchu Bannermen, demanding they find actual productive employment instead of living off the state.

## The Reaction
* This was a fatal political miscalculation. The reforms now directly threatened the literal livelihoods and ancestral privileges of the ruling Manchu elite. 
* Furious and panicked, the Manchu nobility flocked to Empress Dowager Cixi's summer palace, begging her to intervene and stop the Emperor before they were all impoverished.
