# Establishment of the Eight Banners

**Date:** 1615

## Historical Context
Nurhaci, the chieftain of the Jianzhou Jurchens, finalized the consolidation of the Jurchen tribes by establishing the Eight Banners (Ba Qi) system. This military and social organization united the disparate tribes into a formidable fighting force and a cohesive society. The Eight Banners became the core of the Manchu military machine that would eventually conquer the Ming dynasty.

## Kindred Implications
The unification of the mortal tribes mirrored a profound shift in the Kindred society of Manchuria. Oppressed neonates and ancillae, primarily of Clans Gangrel and Tzimisce, formalized the "Vaulderie Pact." This blood ritual broke the chains of their ancient, tyrannical sires. By organizing themselves into hunting packs mirroring the mortal Banners, the younger Manchu vampires became a unified, terrifying force, preparing to ride south and claim their own domain in China.
