# Execution of the Six Gentlemen (September 28, 1898)

Following the Wuxu Coup and the escape of Kang and Liang, Empress Dowager Cixi exacted swift and brutal vengeance on the reformist leaders who failed to escape Peking.

## The Arrests
* Qing troops arrested six prominent reformist scholars and officials: Tan Sitong, Kang Guangren (Kang Youwei's brother), Lin Xu, Yang Shenxiu, Yang Rui, and Liu Guangdi.
* **Tan Sitong's Martyrdom:** Unlike the others, the brilliant philosopher and martial artist Tan Sitong actively refused opportunities to flee. He famously declared: *"In all countries, no reform has ever succeeded without bloodshed. In China, no one has yet shed blood for reform. Let it begin with me."*

## The Executions
* Denied a trial or the ability to defend themselves, the six men were transported in wooden carts to the Caishikou Execution Grounds in the southern part of Peking.
* A large crowd gathered to watch. Tan Sitong remained defiant to the end, shouting at the executioners that he was dying for his country.
* All six were publicly beheaded. 

## The Legacy
* The "Six Gentlemen of Wuxu" became instant martyrs for the cause of Chinese modernization. 
* Their brutal, extrajudicial murders alienated moderate Chinese intellectuals, proving to many that the Qing Dynasty could not be reformed peacefully from within, and sowing the early seeds for the violent republican revolutions that would eventually topple the empire in 1911.
