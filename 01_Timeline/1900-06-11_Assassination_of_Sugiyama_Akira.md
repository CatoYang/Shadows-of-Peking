# Assassination of Sugiyama Akira (June 11, 1900)

While the murder of German Minister von Ketteler on June 20 is more famous, the first diplomat to be killed was the Chancellor of the Japanese Legation, Sugiyama Akira.

## The Murder
* Sugiyama was traveling in a cart toward the Yongding Gate (the southern gate of the Chinese City) to await the arrival of the Seymour Expedition.
* He was intercepted not by Boxers, but by the Kansu Braves—fiercely loyal, anti-foreign Muslim troops under the command of General Dong Fuxiang.
* The soldiers dragged Sugiyama from his cart and hacked him to death, mutilating his body in the street.

## Local Political Impact
* This event proved that the regular Qing Imperial army (specifically the Kansu Braves) was entirely out of control and actively participating in the violence alongside the Boxers.
* It caused outright terror in the Legation Quarter, as it became clear that their diplomatic immunity meant nothing to the troops guarding the city.
