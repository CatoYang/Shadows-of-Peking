# Abolition of the Eight-Legged Essay (June 13, 1898)

One of the very first and most socially disruptive edicts issued by Emperor Guangxu struck at the absolute core of the Chinese intellectual and political system: the Civil Service Examinations.

## The Edict
* For centuries, the only way to achieve political power and government employment in China was by passing the brutal Imperial Examinations. These exams strictly required mastering the "Eight-Legged Essay"—a highly rigid, stylized format focusing entirely on Confucian classics and ancient philosophy.
* On June 13, Guangxu abruptly abolished the Eight-Legged Essay. He ordered that future examinations would test practical knowledge, modern science, economics, and current events.

## The Backlash
* While modernizers cheered, this edict instantly alienated the vast majority of the gentry class. 
* Millions of traditional scholars who had spent their entire lives (and family fortunes) memorizing the archaic texts suddenly found their life's work rendered completely useless overnight. This created a massive, resentful conservative base that enthusiastically supported Empress Dowager Cixi's subsequent coup.
