# Execution of the Five Ministers (July - August 1900)

During the height of the siege, the hardline Pro-Boxer faction led by Prince Duan took absolute control of the Qing court, instituting a reign of terror against their political rivals.

## The Purge
* Five highly prominent, moderate Qing officials—Xu Jingcheng, Yuan Chang, Lishan, Xu Yongyi, and Liankuan—had continuously petitioned the Empress Dowager to stop the attack on the legations, warning it would destroy the empire.
* Enraged by their opposition, Prince Duan accused them of treason and colluding with the foreigners.
* Cixi ordered their execution. They were publicly beheaded at the Caishikou Execution Grounds in Peking (Xu and Yuan on July 28; the others on August 11).

## Political Fallout
* Their executions silenced all remaining moderate voices in the court. 
* However, after the Eight-Nation Alliance captured Peking, the foreign powers demanded these ministers be posthumously exonerated and honored, serving as a massive humiliation for the surviving Qing court.
