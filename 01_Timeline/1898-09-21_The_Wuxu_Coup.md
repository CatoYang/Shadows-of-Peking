# The Wuxu Coup (September 21, 1898)

As the Hundred Days' Reform progressed, the terrified conservative faction rallied around Empress Dowager Cixi, pleading with her to intervene before the Emperor destroyed the traditional system.

## The Plot and the Coup
* Realizing Cixi was moving against him, Emperor Guangxu and his reformers attempted a desperate counter-plot. They reached out to Yuan Shikai, commander of the modernized Beiyang Army, ordering him to arrest Cixi's loyalists and protect the Emperor.
* Instead, the pragmatic Yuan Shikai betrayed the reformers, revealing the plot to Cixi's closest ally, General Ronglu.
* **September 21, 1898:** Cixi struck swiftly. She launched a bloodless palace coup, placing Emperor Guangxu under strict house arrest in the Ocean Terrace, a small island pavilion within the Imperial City.

## The Aftermath
* Cixi declared herself regent once again. She immediately rescinded almost all of the reform edicts.
* **The Six Gentlemen:** While leaders Kang Youwei and Liang Qichao managed to flee to Japan with the help of foreign diplomats, six prominent reformists (including Tan Sitong) were arrested and publicly beheaded at the Caishikou execution ground.
* The coup cemented the power of the hyper-conservative, anti-foreign Manchu faction, setting the political trajectory that would lead directly to the dynasty's alliance with the Boxers.
