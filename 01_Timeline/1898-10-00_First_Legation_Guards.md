# Arrival of the First Legation Guards (October 1898)

In the immediate aftermath of Empress Dowager Cixi's Wuxu Coup against Emperor Guangxu, rumors swirled in Peking that foreigners would be targeted next or that the court was collapsing.

## The Deployment
* The foreign ministers in Peking felt their lives were in danger and unilaterally requested military protection. 
* Small detachments of foreign troops (British, Russian, and German marines) were brought up from the coast to guard the Legation Quarter.

## The Tension
* This was the first time foreign military forces were actively garrisoned inside the walls of Peking. 
* Their presence enraged the local populace and the Qing military, particularly the aggressive Gansu Braves stationed nearby. 
* Though these guards were eventually withdrawn in early 1899 as tensions temporarily cooled, the precedent had been set, and the profound mutual distrust between the legations and the city remained.
