# The Southeast Mutual Protection Pact (June 26, 1900)

When Empress Dowager Cixi issued her June 21 edict declaring war on the Eight-Nation Alliance, the powerful Han Chinese provincial governors realized it was a suicidal move that would lead to the destruction of the Empire.

## The Secret Agreement
* Led by influential viceroys like Li Hongzhang (Viceroy of Liangguang) and Zhang Zhidong (Viceroy of Huguang), the southern and eastern provinces engaged in open insubordination.
* They negotiated directly with foreign consuls in Shanghai and signed a secret pact.
* **The Terms:** The Chinese governors agreed to forcefully suppress the Boxers in their provinces and protect all foreign lives and property. In exchange, the foreign powers agreed not to land troops or wage war in those provinces.

## The Isolation of Peking
* This pact effectively quarantined the Boxer Rebellion to the northern provinces (primarily Zhili, Shandong, and Shanxi). 
* It ensured that the wealthy, modernized south remained completely stable, while turning Peking into an isolated, bloody warzone cut off from the rest of the nation.
