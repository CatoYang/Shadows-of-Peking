# The Escape of Kang Youwei and Liang Qichao (September 22, 1898)

As Empress Dowager Cixi launched the Wuxu Coup on September 21, placing the Emperor under house arrest, a frantic manhunt began for the intellectual architects of the reforms: Kang Youwei and his brilliant student Liang Qichao.

## The Escapes
* **Kang Youwei:** Having received a secret warning from the Emperor just before the coup, Kang fled Peking by train to Tianjin, and then to Shanghai. When Qing agents tried to arrest him in Shanghai, the British intervened, smuggling him onto a British gunboat and escorting him safely to Hong Kong.
* **Liang Qichao:** Liang fled directly into the Japanese Legation in Peking. The Japanese ambassador harbored him, disguised him as a hunter, and smuggled him by boat out of Tianjin to exile in Japan.

## Consequences
* **Cixi's Paranoia:** The fact that the British and Japanese actively intervened to save the two men Cixi considered the empire's greatest traitors deeply enraged the Empress Dowager.
* It permanently cemented her belief that the foreign powers were conspiring with Han Chinese rebels to overthrow her and dismantle the Qing Dynasty, heavily influencing her later decision to ally with the violently anti-foreign Boxers.
* Kang Youwei would go on to found the "Society to Protect the Emperor" in exile, constantly plotting to overthrow Cixi.
