# The Heir Apparent Crisis (January 24, 1900)

Empress Dowager Cixi, seeking to formally depose the house-arrested Emperor Guangxu, appointed a young boy named Pujun as the "Heir Apparent."

## The Political Maneuver
* Pujun was the son of Prince Duan, a fiercely conservative and virulently anti-foreign Manchu prince who heavily patronized the Boxers.
* By making Prince Duan's son the heir, Cixi brought the pro-Boxer faction directly into the inner circle of Imperial succession.

## The Foreign Rebuke
* The foreign ministers in Peking universally saw this as a move to completely crush any remaining reformist/pro-Western elements in the court. 
* In a coordinated slight, all the foreign diplomats refused to send official congratulations to the court on the appointment of the new heir.
* **Impact:** This deeply insulted and infuriated Empress Dowager Cixi and Prince Duan. Prince Duan took this as a personal vendetta against the foreigners and began actively steering the Boxers toward Peking to exterminate them.
