# Kindred Master List

This document catalogs the major Kindred active, torpid, or destroyed within the Shadows of Peking chronicle. They are organized by their primary faction.

---

## The Ancestors (Manchuria)
Ancient Manchu elders who orchestrated the Vaulderie Pact but chose to remain in the pristine northern wilderness rather than rule the conquered South.

1. **Dorgon "The Unbroken"** 
   - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
   - **Status:** Torpor (Changbai Mountains)
   - **Sire:** Unknown ancient 
   - **Childer:** Nurgaci
   - **History:** The original blood-sorcerer who codified the Vaulderie rituals that broke the Manchu neonates from their ancient chains. 

2. **Abahai** 
   - **Clan:** Gangrel 
   - **Status:** Active (Manchuria)
   - **Sire:** Unknown 
   - **Childer:** Oboi the Elder
   - **History:** An ancient watcher of the northern forests, fiercely protective of the ancestral lands.

3. **Nurgaci** 
   - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
   - **Status:** Destroyed (1722, Yongzheng Purges)
   - **Sire:** Dorgon 
   - **Childer:** Jirgalang, Dodo
   - **History:** Named after the mortal Nurhaci, he was the chief architect of the invasion of Ming China. He ruled Peking from the shadows until he was assassinated in a massive purge by his own grand-childer.

4. **Oboi the Elder** 
   - **Clan:** Gangrel 
   - **Status:** Missing
   - **Sire:** Abahai 
   - **Childer:** Ebilun
   - **History:** The beast of the north who rode south, his brutal methods during the 1661 Regency alienated the court. He vanished shortly after the Kangxi Emperor's mortal coup.

5. **Cuyen** 
   - **Clan:** Nosferatu 
   - **Status:** Active (Hidden)
   - **Sire:** Unknown 
   - **Childer:** Amin
   - **History:** The unseen eye of the Manchu invasion, providing scouting and intelligence that crippled the Ming defenses.

## The Conquerors (Peking Court)
The Manchu vampires who successfully invaded Ming China and established the Qing Kindred Court in Peking. They enforce strict traditionalism.

6. **Prince Jirgalang** 
   - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
   - **Status:** Active (Prince of Peking)
   - **Sire:** Nurgaci 
   - **Childer:** Yinzhen, Heshen
   - **History:** The ruthless current Praxis holder of Peking. He maintains power by playing his childer and rivals against one another, utilizing the Boxer Rebellion to purge foreign influence.

7. **Dodo the Butcher** 
   - **Clan:** Gangrel 
   - **Status:** Active (Sheriff of Peking)
   - **Sire:** Nurgaci 
   - **Childer:** Yinreng
   - **History:** The enforcer of the 1645 Queue Order. He personally hunted down hundreds of Han Kindred who failed to shave their heads, learning to track them by the scent of their vitae.

8. **Ebilun** 
   - **Clan:** Gangrel 
   - **Status:** Torpor (Beneath the Forbidden City)
   - **Sire:** Oboi the Elder 
   - **Childer:** None
   - **History:** Deposed during the Yongzheng purges, he was forced into torpor as a compromise to prevent an all-out civil war.

9. **Yinzhen (The Shadow Yongzheng)** 
   - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
   - **Status:** Active (Hidden)
   - **Sire:** Jirgalang 
   - **Childer:** Qianlong
   - **History:** The master of spies. He orchestrated the massive 1722 purges using the mortal secret police to eliminate his Kindred rivals, cementing his sire's rule.

10. **Qianlong** 
    - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Yinzhen 
    - **Childer:** Heseri, Ronglu
    - **History:** A grand patron of the arts and incredibly influential elder. He views the mortal Qing empire's decline with deep sorrow but refuses to relinquish control.

11. **Amin** 
    - **Clan:** Nosferatu 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Cuyen 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The Master of Secrets in Peking. He controls the vast network of beggars and informants throughout the capital.

12. **Heseri** 
    - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Qianlong 
    - **Childer:** Yehenara
    - **History:** The Keeper of Elysium, which is held within the outer courts of the Forbidden City.

13. **Yehenara** 
    - **Clan:** Tzimisce 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Heseri 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The true Kindred power behind the mortal Empress Dowager Cixi. She manipulates the mortal court to protect Manchu supremacy.

14. **Ronglu** 
    - **Clan:** Gangrel 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Qianlong 
    - **Childer:** Yikuang
    - **History:** Commander of the Kindred Banner armies, he organizes the defense of the city against the encroaching European Camarilla.

15. **Yikuang** 
    - **Clan:** Gangrel 
    - **Status:** Active 
    - **Sire:** Ronglu 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** A deeply corrupt enforcer who uses the Boxers to extort mortals and Kindred alike.

## The Subjugated (Han Survivors)
Ancient Han vampires who survived the 1644 fall of Peking. Many fled to Nanjing, while others hide in the shadows of the north.

16. **Zhu Yijun (Wanli)** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Torpor (Nanjing)
    - **Sire:** Unknown Ming Elder 
    - **Childer:** Zhu Youlang
    - **History:** An ancient Ming Emperor who was embraced to prolong his rule. He fled to Nanjing in 1644 and entered torpor shortly after the Southern Ming collapsed.

17. **Zhu Youlang** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1662, Burma)
    - **Sire:** Zhu Yijun 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The last major figure of the Southern Ming resistance. He was hunted down by Dodo the Butcher and destroyed.

18. **Li Zicheng** 
    - **Clan:** Brujah 
    - **Status:** Active (The South)
    - **Sire:** Unknown 
    - **Childer:** Zhang Xianzhong, Tan Sitong (secretly)
    - **History:** The mortal rebel who took Peking in 1644. He was "killed" but actually embraced. He now roams the south as a fiery warlord plotting the Qing's downfall.

19. **Zhang Xianzhong** 
    - **Clan:** Brujah 
    - **Status:** Destroyed
    - **Sire:** Li Zicheng 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The "Demon of Sichuan," a horrific mass murderer whose rampages brought the wrath of both Manchu and Han Kindred down upon him.

20. **Gu Yanwu** 
    - **Clan:** Toreador 
    - **Status:** Active (Peking)
    - **Sire:** Unknown Ming Toreador 
    - **Childer:** Huang Zongxi, Cao Xueqin
    - **History:** A Ming loyalist scholar hiding in plain sight in Peking, preserving the old Han culture.

21. **Huang Zongxi** 
    - **Clan:** Toreador 
    - **Status:** Active (The South)
    - **Sire:** Gu Yanwu 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** A scholar in the south coordinating resistance movements and philosophical opposition to the Qing.

22. **Chen Jinnan** 
    - **Clan:** Malkavian 
    - **Status:** Active
    - **Sire:** Unknown 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The prophetic founder of the secret Heaven and Earth Society (Tiandihui) among Kindred, endlessly plotting to restore the Ming.

23. **Wu Sangui** 
    - **Clan:** Lasombra 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1678)
    - **Sire:** Unknown 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The great traitor who opened Shanhai Pass to the Manchus. He later rebelled against them and was destroyed by Ronglu.

## The Imperial Eunuchs/Servants
Han neonates embraced by the Manchu or surviving Ming elders to serve as daytime managers and spies within the mortal palace.

24. **Wei Zhongxian** 
    - **Clan:** Nosferatu 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1722, Yongzheng Purges)
    - **Sire:** Unknown Ming Nosferatu 
    - **Childer:** Li Lianying, An Dehai
    - **History:** The infamous Ming eunuch who survived 1644 by betraying his Han masters to the Manchu. He was eventually purged when he grew too powerful.

25. **Li Lianying** 
    - **Clan:** Nosferatu 
    - **Status:** Active (Peking)
    - **Sire:** Wei Zhongxian 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Chief eunuch to Empress Dowager Cixi and the primary ghoul/childe serving Yehenara.

26. **An Dehai** 
    - **Clan:** Nosferatu 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1869)
    - **Sire:** Wei Zhongxian 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** An ambitious eunuch executed for leaving the capital without permission, a hit ordered by the Gangrel faction to check Nosferatu power.

27. **Cao Xueqin** 
    - **Clan:** Toreador 
    - **Status:** Active
    - **Sire:** Gu Yanwu 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Embraced to chronicle the tragic decline of the Qing era, he authored *Dream of the Red Chamber*. He is deeply depressed by his immortality.

28. **Sun Yaoting** 
    - **Clan:** Malkavian 
    - **Status:** Active
    - **Sire:** Unknown 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** A young, recently embraced servant plagued by horrific visions of the empire's imminent collapse.

## The Taiping Remnants (Christian Vampires)
Vampires heavily involved in the Taiping Rebellion, deeply tied to the "Er Maozi" (Secondary Devils) and hated by the Peking Court.

29. **Hong Rengan** 
    - **Clan:** Lasombra 
    - **Status:** Active (Hidden)
    - **Sire:** Father Mateo 
    - **Childer:** Li Xiucheng
    - **History:** The shield of the Taiping, he survived the 1864 fall of Nanjing and operates a massive shadow network of Christian Kindred.

30. **Li Xiucheng (The Loyal King)** 
    - **Clan:** Lasombra 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1864)
    - **Sire:** Hong Rengan 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Executed during the fall of Nanjing, fighting a desperate last stand against the Manchu Gangrel.

31. **Yang Xiuqing (The Voice of God)** 
    - **Clan:** Malkavian 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1856)
    - **Sire:** Unknown Malkavian 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Claimed to speak for the Christian God. His madness threatened the rebellion and he was purged by his own kind in the Tianjing Incident.

32. **Shi Dakai (The Winged King)** 
    - **Clan:** Brujah 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1863)
    - **Sire:** Unknown Brujah 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Broke away from the main Taiping forces due to internal Kindred politics, eventually cornered and executed by the Qing army.

33. **Wang Tao** 
    - **Clan:** Toreador 
    - **Status:** Active (Shanghai)
    - **Sire:** European Toreador 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** A Christian reformer and journalist bridging East and West, operating safely in the foreign concessions of Shanghai.

## The Foreign Envoys (Camarilla)
European Kindred residing in the Legation Quarter, attempting to enforce the Camarilla's traditions and induct the Chinese vampires.

34. **Lord Thomas Wade** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Active (Legation Quarter)
    - **Sire:** Lord Palmerston (London) 
    - **Childer:** Claude MacDonald, Kang Youwei
    - **History:** The primary Camarilla Envoy to Peking. He believes he can "civilize" the Qing Court but is currently trapped by the Boxers.

35. **Sir Claude MacDonald** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Active (Legation Quarter)
    - **Sire:** Thomas Wade 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The military commander of the Legation Kindred, organizing the defense against Ronglu's Banner armies.

36. **Heinrich von Ketteler** 
    - **Clan:** Tremere 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1900)
    - **Sire:** Karl Schrekt (Vienna) 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** An arrogant German envoy whose public assassination by mortal forces (and secret orchestration by Amin) sparked the Siege of the Legations.

37. **Isabella Bird** 
    - **Clan:** Toreador 
    - **Status:** Active
    - **Sire:** Unknown British Toreador 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** An explorer and chronicler who travels the interior, observing the Boxer Rebellion's impact on rural Kindred.

38. **Father Mateo** 
    - **Clan:** Lasombra antitribu 
    - **Status:** Missing
    - **Sire:** Unknown 
    - **Childer:** Hong Rengan
    - **History:** A Jesuit priest who arrived centuries ago. He sired the first Taiping vampires before vanishing into the Chinese interior.

## The Reformers
Younger Kindred influenced by the 1898 Hundred Days Reform, attempting to overthrow the conservative Manchu elders.

39. **Kang Youwei** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Active (Exiled in Japan)
    - **Sire:** Lord Thomas Wade 
    - **Childer:** Liang Qichao, Guangxu (secretly)
    - **History:** The visionary of the mortal Reform Movement. Embraced by the British to force modernization. He fled to Japan after the Wuxu Coup failed.

40. **Liang Qichao** 
    - **Clan:** Ventrue 
    - **Status:** Active (Exiled in Japan)
    - **Sire:** Kang Youwei 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** Kang's brilliant student, continuing the ideological fight from abroad.

41. **Tan Sitong** 
    - **Clan:** Brujah 
    - **Status:** Destroyed (1898, The Six Gentlemen)
    - **Sire:** Li Zicheng (secretly) 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** A mortal reformer who was secretly embraced right before his execution. He chose to meet the sun rather than flee, becoming a martyr.

42. **Guangxu (The Emperor's Shadow)** 
    - **Clan:** Malkavian 
    - **Status:** Torpor (Ocean Terrace, Forbidden City)
    - **Sire:** Kang Youwei (botched) 
    - **Childer:** None
    - **History:** The mortal Guangxu Emperor was placed under house arrest. In a desperate bid, Kang Youwei attempted to embrace him, but the vitae reacted poorly with the Emperor's fragile state, turning him into a Malkavian and driving him into torpor.
