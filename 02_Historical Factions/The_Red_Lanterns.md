# The Red Lanterns (Hongdengzhao)

The Red Lanterns were the female counterpart and sister-organization to the Boxers. Because Boxer ideology forbade men from interacting with women (believing female "yin" energy would pollute their magic and break their invulnerability), women formed their own parallel militias.

## Beliefs and Magic
* The Red Lanterns were comprised mostly of teenage girls and young unmarried women. They dressed entirely in red, carried red lanterns, and wielded red fans and swords.
* **Supreme Magic:** Within the movement's lore, the Red Lanterns were believed to possess magic far more powerful than the men. It was widely believed by the populace and the Boxers alike that the Red Lanterns could walk on water, fly through the air to burn foreign churches with their fans, and heal the wounded.
* **Lin Hei'er (The Yellow Lotus Holy Mother):** The most famous leader of the Red Lanterns in Tianjin. She commanded such immense respect that even high-ranking Qing officials would kneel in the dirt as her palanquin passed.

## Societal Impact
* In a deeply patriarchal Confucian society where women were traditionally confined to the home and subjected to foot-binding, the Red Lanterns briefly inverted the social order. For a few short months in 1900, peasant girls commanded absolute awe and authority in the streets of Peking and Tianjin.
