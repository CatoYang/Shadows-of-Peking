# The Righteous and Harmonious Fists (The Boxers)

The *Yihetuan* (Boxers) were a decentralized, grassroots peasant movement born out of devastating floods, drought, and economic displacement in northern China.

## Structure and Beliefs
* **Decentralized:** They had no single supreme leader. They operated in local village bands, usually led by charismatic martial artists or spirit mediums.
* **Spirit Possession:** They believed that through martial arts rituals, incantations, and swallowing charms, they could invite Daoist gods and legendary heroes to possess their bodies, making them completely invulnerable to foreign bullets and swords.
* **Demographics:** Mostly impoverished young men, displaced farmers, and unemployed transport workers whose livelihoods had been destroyed by foreign-built railways and steamships. There were also female counterparts, notably the "Red Lanterns."

## Motivations and Context
* **Anti-Christian and Anti-Foreign:** They blamed the drought and their poverty entirely on foreign "devils" disrupting the Feng Shui of the land with railways and churches, and on the "Secondary Devils" (Chinese Christians) who abandoned traditional rituals.
* **Political Shift:** Initially an anti-Qing secret society, they shifted their rallying cry to "Support the Qing, Destroy the Foreigners" to avoid being crushed by the Imperial army, resulting in an uneasy alliance with the Manchu court.
