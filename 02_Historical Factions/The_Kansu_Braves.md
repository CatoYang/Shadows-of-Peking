# The Kansu Braves (Gansu Army)

While often lumped in with the general "Imperial Army," the Kansu Braves (*Gan Jun*) were a distinct, highly terrifying faction in their own right, operating with a high degree of violent autonomy in Peking.

## Composition and Command
* **General Dong Fuxiang:** A former Muslim rebel who had surrendered to the Qing and become an exceptionally brutal and loyal Imperial general. 
* **The Troops:** The army consisted of roughly 10,000 hardened, predominantly Chinese Muslim (Hui) soldiers from the remote western province of Gansu. They wore distinct uniforms and were fiercely, militantly anti-foreign.

## Role in the Rebellion
* Unlike the modernized Beiyang troops (who often fired over the heads of the foreigners) or the Manchu Bannermen (who were relatively ineffective), the Kansu Braves were utterly ruthless. 
* They were responsible for the first major escalation in Peking by dragging Japanese Chancellor Sugiyama Akira from his cart and hacking him to death.
* They were the primary military force besieging the Legation Quarter and were responsible for deliberately setting fire to the ancient Hanlin Academy. The foreigners feared the Kansu Braves far more than they feared the Boxers.
