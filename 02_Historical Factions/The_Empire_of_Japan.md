# The Empire of Japan

Japan was the rising power in East Asia. Having rapidly modernized during the Meiji Restoration, they had shocked the world by decisively crushing the Qing Dynasty in the 1894-1895 Sino-Japanese War.

## Motivations and Context
* **Seeking Equality:** Japan desperately wanted to be recognized as a "civilized" great power on equal footing with the Western empires. They saw the Boxer Rebellion as a prime opportunity to prove their military prowess and reliability to the West.
* **Imperial Expansion:** Japan wanted to undermine Chinese sovereignty to expand its own sphere of influence, particularly eyeing control over Korea and Taiwan (which they already possessed), and pushing against Russian influence in Manchuria.
* **The Military Contingent:** Japan provided the largest, most disciplined, and most effective military contingent to the Eight-Nation Alliance (over 20,000 troops). Their soldiers were instrumental in taking Tianjin and breaching the walls of Peking.
