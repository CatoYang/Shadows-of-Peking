# The Han Moderates and Provincial Leaders

While the Manchu nobility controlled the court in Peking, much of the actual military and economic power in China was held by powerful Han Chinese provincial governors and viceroys. 

## Key Figures
* **Li Hongzhang:** Viceroy of Zhili (historically) and a massive political force. He recognized the vast military superiority of the West and constantly advocated for diplomacy over war.
* **Yuan Shikai:** A brilliant, ruthless, and highly ambitious Han general. He commanded the Beiyang Army (the only fully modernized, Western-trained army in China). As Governor of Shandong, he violently crushed the Boxers, driving them north to Peking.
* **Zhang Zhidong & Liu Kunyi:** Powerful southern viceroys who championed the "Self-Strengthening Movement" (modernizing military and industry while keeping traditional Chinese philosophy).

## Motivations and Context
* **The Southeast Mutual Protection:** When Cixi declared war on the foreign powers in June 1900, these Han leaders actively ignored the imperial decree. They struck a secret deal with foreign consuls, agreeing to suppress the Boxers in their provinces and protect foreigners in exchange for the foreign powers not invading the south.
* **The Han/Manchu Split:** The Han moderates believed the Manchu court was committing suicide by backing the Boxers. They wanted to modernize the nation and implement constitutional reforms, putting them at extreme odds with the Manchu conservatives in the capital.
