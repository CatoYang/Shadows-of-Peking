# The Chinese Christians (The "Secondary Devils")

Often overlooked as passive victims, the Chinese Christians were an active, crucial faction during the siege of Peking. The Boxers derogatorily referred to them as *Er Maozi* ("Secondary Devils" or "Second-Class Hairy Men").

## The Siege
* When the Boxers entered Peking, thousands of Chinese Christians were massacred in the streets.
* Roughly 3,000 fled into the Legation Quarter, and another 3,000 sought refuge in the Beitang (Northern Cathedral).
* **Crucial Labor:** The foreign diplomats initially did not want to let them into the Legations, fearing they would drain food supplies. However, the Chinese Christians became the sole labor force of the defense. They dug the trenches, built the massive barricades under heavy fire, and reinforced the walls. Without their grueling labor, the Legation Quarter would have fallen within days.

## Motivations
* They were a deeply polarizing group. While many converted for genuine faith or for the food/protection offered by missionaries, their refusal to participate in traditional village rituals (like rainmaking festivals) made them social pariahs. By the time of the siege, they were fighting purely for their lives, knowing surrender meant immediate beheading.
