# The Russian Empire

Russia was pursuing an aggressive, expansionist policy in the Far East, driven by the desire for warm-water ports that would not freeze in the winter.

## Motivations and Context
* **The Prize of Manchuria:** Russia's primary goal was not punishing the Boxers in Peking, but using the chaos as a pretext to seize complete control over Manchuria (northeastern China), the ancestral homeland of the Qing dynasty.
* **The Invasion:** While allied forces marched on Peking, Russia launched a massive separate invasion, pouring 100,000 troops into Manchuria.
* **Geopolitical Rivalry:** Russia's aggressive land grab in Manchuria terrified the Japanese and alarmed the British. This direct clash of imperial ambitions during the Boxer Rebellion directly set the stage for the catastrophic Russo-Japanese War of 1904-1905.
