# The Manchu Court Conservatives (The Ironhats)

At the pinnacle of the Qing Dynasty's power structure sat the Manchu nobility. The Manchus were an ethnic minority who had conquered China in 1644, and by 1900, they were deeply paranoid about losing their grip on power to both foreign imperialists and the Han Chinese majority.

## Key Figures
* **Empress Dowager Cixi:** The pragmatic and ruthless de facto ruler of China. She balanced factions against each other but ultimately sided with the conservatives to maintain Manchu supremacy.
* **Prince Duan (Aisin Gioro Zaiyi):** A fiercely anti-foreign Manchu prince and the leading patron of the Boxers at court. He convinced Cixi that the Boxers were a divine weapon to expel the foreigners.
* **General Dong Fuxiang:** Commander of the Kansu Braves, a fiercely loyal and violent army of Muslim troops from western China who despised foreigners and eagerly participated in the siege.

## Motivations and Context
* **The Ethnic Divide:** The conservative Manchus distrusted the Han Chinese reformers, viewing modernization as a Western poison that would dilute Manchu traditional authority.
* **Desperation:** Following the humiliation of the Sino-Japanese War and the Scramble for Concessions, the Manchu nobles felt backed into a corner. They backed the Boxers not out of modern nationalism, but as a desperate, traditionalist attempt to preserve the Qing dynasty and their own elite status.
