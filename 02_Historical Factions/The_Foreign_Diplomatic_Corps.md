# The Foreign Diplomatic Corps and Missionaries

The foreign presence in Peking was concentrated in the Legation Quarter, a small, fortified neighborhood near the Forbidden City.

## Key Factions Within
* **The Diplomats:** Led by figures like British Minister Claude MacDonald and the aggressively undiplomatic German Minister Clemens von Ketteler. They were accustomed to dictating terms to the Qing government through "gunboat diplomacy."
* **The Missionaries:** Operating heavily in the countryside, they frequently clashed with local customs. Catholic missionaries in particular often arrogantly assumed the rank of local magistrates to protect their Chinese converts in lawsuits, deeply enraging the local populace.

## Motivations and Context
* **Arrogance and Miscalculation:** The diplomats lived in a bubble of perceived racial and military superiority. They largely ignored the warnings of the growing Boxer threat until the militias were literally burning the churches in the city.
* **Survival:** Once the siege began, this disparate group of rival nations had to overcome their mutual distrust to organize a desperate defense of the Legation Quarter to avoid being massacred.
