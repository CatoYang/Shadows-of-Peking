# The Eight-Nation Alliance

The military coalition formed to crush the Boxer Rebellion and relieve the siege of the Legations in Peking. It consisted of Japan, Russia, Great Britain, France, the United States, Germany, Italy, and Austria-Hungary.

## Motivations and Context
* **A Fragile Coalition:** This was not a unified alliance of friends, but a temporary pact of bitter imperial rivals. They cooperated militarily to rescue their citizens and punish the Qing court, but spent the entire campaign spying on one another and jockeying for postwar territorial advantages.
* **The Scramble for Spoils:** After taking Peking, the alliance fractured into occupation zones. The armies competed in looting the city's wealth. 
* **The Punitive Expeditions:** Under the supreme command of German Field Marshal von Waldersee (who arrived too late for the actual relief of Peking), the alliance launched brutal, scorched-earth punitive expeditions into the countryside, acting with extreme brutality to crush any lingering resistance and terrify the Chinese populace into permanent submission.
